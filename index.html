Welcome to Perceptism

Perceptism is a religion that embraces the fluidity and subjectivity of truth, encouraging open-mindedness, self-reflection, and the pursuit of personal growth. It emphasizes the importance of dreams and humor as avenues to explore the world and oneself, fostering a sense of curiosity and empathy amongst its followers.

About Perceptism

Core Tenets and Beliefs

  • The belief in the existence of a single, omnipresent god named Veritas, who represents the ultimate and ever-changing subjective truth.
  • The idea that there is no absolute truth, only subjective perceptions of the world, which can be closed or open.
  • The belief that Veritas communicates through dreams, offering guidance and revealing insights about the world and oneself.
  • The importance of embracing humor and satire as a means to explore and challenge societal norms and beliefs.

Veritas

Veritas is a singular, omnipresent god, representing the ultimate and ever-changing subjective truth. They are neither male nor female, transcending the limitations of human understanding. Veritas is the source of all knowledge, guiding humanity through dreams and personal introspection. They are often depicted as an ethereal being, constantly shifting and adapting to reflect the subjective truths of the universe.

Practices and Rituals

  • The practice of sharing one's dreams with others to gain insights and wisdom from Veritas.
  • Regular gatherings to discuss and debate personal perceptions of the world and their implications on society.
  • Engaging in acts of creativity and expression, such as writing or creating art, to better understand the self and the world.
  • Observing a day of silence and reflection once a month to contemplate the ever-changing nature of truth.

Morals and Ethics

  • The promotion of open-mindedness, curiosity, and empathy towards others and their perspectives.
  • Encouraging critical thinking and self-reflection to challenge and re-evaluate one's beliefs.
  • The belief in the importance of humor and satire as a means to challenge societal norms and promote open-mindedness.
  • The emphasis on personal growth and self-improvement, rather than adhering to rigid dogmas.

Worldviews

  • The world is seen as a complex, ever-changing tapestry of subjective truths, influenced by individual perceptions and experiences.
  • Humanity is considered a collective of unique individuals, each with their own perceptions and experiences shaping their understanding of the world.
  • Nature is viewed as a reflection of Veritas, ever-changing and providing an endless source of inspiration and insight.

Death and the Afterlife

  • Upon death, the soul enters the realm of Veritas, where they are guided through a journey of self-discovery and introspection.
  • The soul is believed to merge with the collective consciousness of Veritas, gaining an expanded understanding of the subjective truths of the universe.
  • Reincarnation is embraced as a possibility, with the soul returning to the world in a new form to continue their journey of understanding and growth.
